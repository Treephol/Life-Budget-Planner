The longer arc

asset-to-debt ratio · 2017 actual — 2036 projected

Here's the ten-year view, built from the Assets & Debt tab. What we own has compounded about 15.4% a year since 2017. What we owe is now coming down: assuming no new property, the existing loans amortize on their current schedule — the high-interest Cash (Thai Credit) loan clears around 2031, and the rest chip down steadily. Holding both forward, assets pass debt around 2028–29 — and from there the gap widens fast.

Asset-to-debt ratio (actual) Projected (assets +15.4%/yr · debt amortizing) Parity — assets = debt (1.0)

Below 1.0, debt still outweighs assets; above it, the balance has tipped in our favour. The ratio climbed from 0.23 in 2017 to 0.80 in 2023, then dipped in 2024–25 when The Base Cherngtalay added a new mortgage. From today's 0.62 it crosses 1.0 around 2028–29 and keeps climbing as the loans are repaid — reaching roughly 6× by 2036, with assets near ฿19.9M against debt paid down to about ฿3.3M.
